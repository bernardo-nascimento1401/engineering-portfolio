01 · OVERVIEW

Structures work on Penn Rocketry.

My work has included vehicle layout, boat-tail design, finite-element analysis, fiberglass fabrication, and final assembly. These tasks require attention to mass, stiffness, stability, packaging, aerodynamic drag, and manufacturability.

My contribution

  • Designed an eight-inch boat tail integrating the motor-mount interface, external taper, and internal reinforcement.
  • Evaluated design options including aluminum, fiberglass, PETG, and glass-reinforced printed structures.
  • Built finite-element models using predicted launch loads to evaluate stress and deformation.
  • Developed detailed CAD for fasteners, interfaces, clearances, and motor-retention hardware.
  • Designed 3D-printable drill templates to improve radial hole spacing during fabrication.
  • Supported fiberglass airframe manufacturing, hardware integration, and vehicle assembly.

02 · VEHICLE MODEL

Vehicle layout and stability.

Before fabrication, the team used OpenRocket to check component placement, mass, center of gravity, center of pressure, stability margin, recovery layout, and motor selection. I used the model as a reference when checking how structural changes affected the rest of the vehicle.

03 · BOAT-TAIL DEVELOPMENT

Boat-tail design.

I designed Penn Rocketry's first boat-tail concept around the motor mount. The 8-inch taper reduced the outer diameter from 6.17 inches to 4.45 inches while leaving space for the motor case and retention hardware.

The part included internal ribs, a locating lip for the lower body tube, and interfaces for the fasteners and motor mount. I compared aluminum, fiberglass, printed PETG, and glass-reinforced PETG based on heat exposure, stiffness, mass, cost, and build time.

04 · STRUCTURAL VALIDATION

Finite-element analysis.

I used FEA to check whether an additively manufactured boat tail could handle the predicted acceleration, aerodynamic loads, and local interface forces. I applied the expected loads and constraints, then reviewed von Mises stress and deformation.

I used the results to adjust the reinforcement layout and compare material and manufacturing options. The stress plot was one part of the design loop, alongside geometry, interfaces, and the practical limits of fabrication.

05 · MANUFACTURING & INTEGRATION

Fabrication and integration.

I helped drill and assemble fiberglass airframes, place bulkheads, and integrate the motor mount. Because equally spaced radial holes are difficult to lay out by hand, I designed a 3D-printed drill-template sleeve that located the holes consistently around the tube.

06 · OUTCOME

Design, analysis, and fabrication.

This project has given me experience with the full sequence from vehicle requirements to CAD, FEA, material selection, fixtures, fabrication, and assembly. Working on the hardware also helped me recognize which dimensions and interfaces needed more attention in the models.

I especially enjoyed owning both the CAD and analysis work and the practical details needed to build and install the part.
